The wave function in quantum mechanics is a mathematical object used to encode the state of a system.
According to the Born rule, the absolute square of the wave function gives a probability density.
The Schrodinger equation governs the time evolution of the wave function in non-relativistic quantum mechanics.
Operators correspond to observables, and measurement outcomes are associated with eigenvalues of those operators.
The uncertainty principle limits simultaneous knowledge of certain pairs of observables such as position and momentum.
